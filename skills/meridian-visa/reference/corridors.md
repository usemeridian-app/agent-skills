# corridors meridian covers best

hand-tuned notes for the 5 corridors with the deepest validation. defer to `requirements_lookup` for the authoritative current state; this file is context, not source of truth.

## NG → UK (tourism, standard visitor visa)

- **fee**: £127 for 6-month visitor visa (2026 rate). non-refundable, paid before biometrics.
- **processing**: 3 weeks standard, 5 working days priority (£500), 24-hour super-priority (£1,000) where available
- **biometrics**: required at VFS Global, Lagos or Abuja. book early; Lagos slots can run 2-3 weeks out
- **what they want to see**: stable employment + clear ties to NG + sufficient funds. £3,500+ balance is the unofficial floor
- **common rejections**: insufficient funds (most common), poor travel history, unclear sponsor relationship, fabricated documents
- **passport rule**: 6 months past return, 1 blank page
- **photo**: 45x35mm, white background, taken within 6 months
- **insurance**: not required but high-leverage if visiting for health reasons
- **flag if vault shows**: bank balance under £3,500, recent large unexplained deposits, employment letter older than 1 month

## NG → Schengen (tourism, type C short-stay)

- **fee**: €90 for adults, €45 for kids 6-12. some VAC service fees on top (€30-40)
- **processing**: 15 calendar days standard, up to 45 in busy seasons. apply 6+ weeks before travel
- **biometrics**: VFS or TLScontact depending on country. germany via VFS Lagos, france via TLScontact
- **what they want to see**: comprehensive itinerary + €30,000 insurance + return-pull evidence
- **common rejections**: insufficient justification of purpose, missing or inadequate insurance, weak finances, gaps in itinerary
- **insurance**: mandatory. €30,000 minimum cover, valid across full schengen area, full trip dates including arrival and departure
- **first-time applicant**: apply through the country you'll spend the most time in. if equal, country of first entry
- **passport rule**: 3 months past return from schengen area, 2 blank pages
- **photo**: 35x45mm, recent, neutral expression, white background
- **flag if vault shows**: insurance with under €30,000 cover, partial schengen coverage, gaps between flight dates and hotel dates

## KE → US (tourism, B1/B2)

- **fee**: USD 185 (MRV fee), non-refundable
- **processing**: interview wait times vary by post. nairobi typically 6-12 months wait for first-time B1/B2 in 2025-2026
- **interview**: required at US embassy nairobi. dress business, bring originals
- **what they want to see**: strong ties to KE, clear purpose, no prior US compliance issues
- **common rejections**: failure to demonstrate non-immigrant intent (214(b)), unclear funding source, inconsistent statements
- **DS-160**: confirmation page printed and brought to interview. photo uploaded must match specs (US passport photo: 2x2 inches, white background)
- **passport rule**: 6 months past intended stay
- **flag if vault shows**: prior US overstay, vague employment, recent immigrant intent signals (US property, US-based fiancé), DS-160 mismatch with bank statement
- **special note**: 214(b) refusals are not appealable. re-apply only with materially new evidence

## IN → US (tourism, B1/B2)

- **fee**: USD 185 (MRV fee)
- **processing**: interview wait times by post: mumbai, delhi, chennai, kolkata, hyderabad. as of late 2025, B1/B2 wait times improved significantly. dropbox (interview waiver) reinstated for many qualified renewals
- **dropbox eligibility**: prior B1/B2 expired within 48 months, age and prior-visa conditions met
- **what they want to see**: strong domestic ties, clear purpose, sufficient funds, no immigrant intent
- **common rejections**: 214(b), inconsistent purpose narrative, financial gaps, family-based immigrant intent
- **DS-160**: same as KE corridor. ensure photo specs match
- **passport rule**: 6 months past intended stay, 2 blank pages
- **flag if vault shows**: family member in US adjustment-of-status process, prior overstay or visa-issue history, vague self-employment income

## NG → US (tourism, B1/B2)

- **fee**: USD 185 (MRV fee)
- **processing**: lagos and abuja both serve NG. wait times for B1/B2 first-time interviews can run 8-14 months as of late 2025
- **interview**: required. nigerian applicants face higher 214(b) refusal rates than the global average; ties documentation matters more here
- **what they want to see**: deep NG ties (property, family, business, long employment tenure), clear purpose (specific trip, not "exploring"), funding source consistent with stated income
- **common rejections**: 214(b) for weak ties or weak purpose, document inconsistencies (NG documents are scrutinized for fabrication signals), funding gaps
- **DS-160**: photo specs strict. 2x2 inches, white background, recent, no glasses
- **passport rule**: 6 months past intended stay
- **flag if vault shows**: short employment history at current employer, large unexplained deposits, vague trip purpose, prior US refusal without addressing the stated reason
- **special note**: a clean schengen or UK travel history is a meaningful boost to NG→US first-time applications
